Our Equipment

1. Skates: Hockey skates are designed specifically for the sport, providing ankle support and a low-profile design. Choose skates that fit well, ensuring comfort and control on the ice.
2. Helmet: A certified hockey helmet with a cage or shield is crucial for protecting your head and face. Ensure the helmet fits snugly and securely.
3. Protective Gear: Shoulder pads, elbow pads, and shin guards provide protection against impacts. Choose sizes that offer full coverage without restricting movement.

Hockey Training

1. Cardiovascular Conditioning: Hockey is an intense, fast-paced sport, and players require excellent cardiovascular fitness. Incorporate aerobic exercises like running, cycling, and interval training to improve endurance.
2. Strength Training: Develop strength in the core, legs, and upper body to enhance power and stability on the ice. Exercises may include squats, lunges, deadlifts, and upper body workouts.
3. Agility and Speed Training: Agility drills, ladder exercises, and speed training help players improve their footwork, quick changes of direction, and overall speed on the ice.
4. Balance and Coordination: Hockey players need good balance and coordination for effective skating, shooting, and maneuvering. Incorporate balance exercises and drills that enhance overall body control.
5. Stickhandling and Shooting Drills: Regular practice of stickhandling, passing, and shooting is essential. Develop quick hands and accurate shots through targeted drills both on and off the ice.
6. Skating Drills: Focus on improving skating technique, including forward and backward skating, crossovers, and quick starts. Skating drills help players move efficiently and gain a competitive edge.

Private Lessons

1. Personalized Instruction: Private lessons allow for one-on-one attention, enabling you to tailor your instruction to the specific needs and skill level of the individual player. This personalized approach can accelerate skill development.
2. Focused Skill Improvement: Private lessons provide an opportunity to focus intensely on particular aspects of the game, such as skating techniques, stickhandling, shooting accuracy, or position-specific skills.
3. Flexible Scheduling: Offering private lessons allows for flexibility in scheduling. You can work with players to find convenient time slots that fit their schedules, potentially attracting individuals who may not be able to commit to regular team practices.
4. Individualized Feedback: Private lessons offer the chance to provide immediate and detailed feedback. Players can receive personalized tips and corrections, accelerating their progress.
5. Mental Skills Development: Beyond physical skills, private lessons provide an opportunity to work on mental aspects of the game, including strategy, decision-making, and maintaining focus under pressure.
6. Specialized Training Programs: You can design specialized training programs based on the player's goals, whether they aim to improve for recreational play, prepare for competitive leagues, or pursue a professional career.

Booking

1.Ice Time Reservation: Determine the process for reserving ice time for team practices, games, or training sessions. Check if there is a booking system, and if so, understand how to access and use it.
2.Event Booking: If you're planning an event, inquire about the procedure for booking the club's facilities for tournaments, fundraisers, or other activities. Confirm the availability of locker rooms, meeting rooms, and any additional amenities required.
3.Equipment Booking: If the hockey club provides equipment, understand the process for booking or borrowing gear for specific events or practices.
4.Membership and Registration: Ensure that all players or participants have completed the necessary registrations or memberships required for club activities. Check if there are specific booking privileges for members.
5.Payment Information: Understand the payment procedures associated with booking. This could include fees for ice time, event spaces, or equipment rental. Inquire about any deposit requirements and cancellation policies.
